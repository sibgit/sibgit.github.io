Manta ray (Mobula sp.)

Habitat and distribution

Mantas are pelagic species found in tropical and subtropical waters of all of the world's major oceans. They also venture into temperate seas.
The furthest from the equator that mantas have been recorded is North Carolina in the United States (31°N) and the North Island of New Zealand (36°S).
Manta rays prefer water temperatures above 20 °C.

M. alfredi is mainly found in tropical areas.
M. birostris lives mostly in the open ocean, travelling with the currents and migrating to areas where upwellings of nutrient-rich water increase prey concentrations.
