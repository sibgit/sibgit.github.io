Yeti (Orang Pendek)

Habitat and distribution

The yeti is a mythical creature believed to inhabit the Himalayan mountains of Asia, including regions in Nepal, Bhutan, China, India, and Tibet. Its habitat is described as the high-altitude, snowy mountains, but also the forests below the snow line, where it forages for food.
Despite numerous expeditions, the yeti's existence remains unproven by science, with sightings and evidence like footprints often explained as misidentified bears or other animals.
Primary location: The Yeti is most famously associated with the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, including Mount Everest.

The yeti's distribution is confined to the vast and rugged mountain ranges of central and southern Asia. Reports of the creature also extend to parts of central Asia, such as Russia and Mongolia.

Diet / behavior / social organization

Generally described as omnivorous. Vegetation: Consumes roots, fruits, branches, and other vegetation. Animal prey: Hunts animals such as yak and sheep, though reports of aggressive hunting are sometimes debated.
